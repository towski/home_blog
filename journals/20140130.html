Went to a big celebration at github (the hugely popular world changing website for programmers). I wasn't quite sure what to expect. Their new office has been largely empty up to now. But I was impressed when I got there, they had filled the space nicely. They had tons of delicious food, some meat that just melted off the bone. An old friend invited me, and it was good to chat about the ever changing expansive world of github.

It's an impressive world that I admit, I feel somewhat of an outsider in. I'm envious of their sophisticated lifestyle, and I wish I was an Ubermensch too. It's rare to see so many successful people, all bootstrapped by their own ability. We toasted their success. I was talking to a young developer who was enamored by all the celebrity programmers surrounding him. It has taken me a long to understand how meaningful one persons contribution to code can be.

And the party itself seemed to show how github has matured. I've watched the company and website grow immensely since inception. Gone are the raucous drunken parties of the early days, and fewer are the open bars nights in San Francisco and around the world. One of the founders once told me he had visited something like 40 countries promoting github. I didn't realize at the time just how immensely successful github was.

On my way home I noticed that Pop's (bar on 24th and York) was near empty, and someone was staring out the window at people. I went inside, grabbed a beer, and struck up a conversation with the guy. He was friendly, from New York, 46, and admitted that he likes to go into Pop's regularly, have a drink by himself, and listen to his iPod. He left and I chatted to the waitress for a bit. She pointed out that the place was basically empty because there was an impending shutdown. The owner had changed, and as soon as it finalized they would close to remodel. The few remaining patrons were regulars. I enjoyed being there for what seemed like the final moments of something meaningful. The waitress was more saddened by it, and not too excited about what will replace it. Another local haunt bites the dust. I can just imagine someone pausing reflectively as they shut the door for the last time.
